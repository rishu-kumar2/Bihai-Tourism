Laddoo

When the talk is about Bihari cuisine, one cannot forget the laddoo of Maner.

Laddoo is a ball shaped sweet made of gram-flour, sugar and ghee. Though, sold in every sweet shop, the laddoo made in Maner, about 30 km west of Patna, are famous and the best

Malpua

The luscious and mouth watering malpua, which is a speciality of Bihar is prepared from a combination of maida, milk, banana, grated coconut, cashew nut, raisin, sugar, water and green cardamon and fried in ghee.

Lai

Bihar is blessed with all the flavor and spirit of pomp and splendor. There are several varieties of Lai available in Bihar, including Lai from Gaya. The main component of this Lai is Ram dana seeds. These ram danas are processed and mixed with khoya and sugar or jaggery to give rise to a disk-shaped sweet. Rich in nutrients and flavor, lai ka laddoo has several health benefits and is also consumed as a portion of fasting food.

Perukia

Chhapra town of Bihar is particularly famous for Perukia. It is prepared by mixing Sooji (semolina) or khoa with sugar and wrapping the mixture in thin leaves made from a mix of maida, water and ghee. The sweet is then deep fried in ghee and served.

Thekua

Thekua is a traditional deep-fried sweet snack from Bihar made from whole wheat flour (atta). Thekua is also called kajuria, khajoor, and many more names.
The key thekua ingredients are whole wheat flour (atta), ghee, and jaggery (gur or unrefined brown sugar). it is traditionally made with jaggery, which makes it healthy and tasty. You can replace jaggery with regular sugar or honey if you want. When melting jaggery, grate it and use a very small amount of water. This is important to achieve a stiff dough, which is essential for the preparation of the right consistency, thickness, and texture. Coconut, fennel seed, and cardamom are also added to the thekua recipe to enhance the taste, flavor, and aroma of thekua. Thekua is traditionally rolled out, shaped, and pressed against a wooden mold called saancha, which can be greased with ghee to prevent the dough from sticking to it before it is deep-fried.
This traditional Bihari thekua recipe is offered as prasad during the Bihari festival of Chhath Puja (it is considered one of the Chhath Puja special recipes).

Kasar

Kasar is a traditional homemade delicious, sweet prepared on different festive occasions. It is prepared using grinded rice flour, mixed fennel seeds, and ghee. Mix it all together and crush the jaggery into powdered form and add it to the mixture. One can use dry fruits to increase the taste. We can sprinkle some milk and grease your hand with some ghee before making small round ladoos. Now kasar is ready to relish.
Its mouth-watering taste will make you want more and more of it. Although you can eat it anytime, this dish is best enjoyed during chhath puja. It is beneficial to consume it in the winter season.

Laung Lata

Famous Bihari regional sweet laung Latta has many names like lavang lata, laung Latika, etc. Laung Lata is crispy on the outer surface and a little juicy inside. The outer layer is made using all-purpose flour and it is filled with a mix of milk mava and nuts. The outer layer is sealed using lavang (clove) which gives an exotic aroma to it. When served warm it tastes awesome. The richness of mava, nuts, and ghee gives a delicious taste.
You will find this dish famous by the name of Laung Lata in almost all the sweet shops of Bihar. The clove of this crusty crunchy dessert sets it apart from others. As soon as you keep it in your mouth, the strong taste of clove will melt in the whole mouth with a sweetness flavor.
